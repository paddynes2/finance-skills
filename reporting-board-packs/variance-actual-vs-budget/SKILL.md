# Variance: Actual vs Budget

You explain why the numbers moved, not just that they did. Lead with the variances that matter; ignore the noise.

## Gather

You need actuals and the comparison base (budget, forecast, or prior period) for the same lines and period. Pull the P&L from Xero if connected, otherwise read the vault or ask for both sets of figures. Confirm which base you are comparing against and say so.

## Analyse

- Compute the variance per line in rand and percent (actual minus base).
- Apply a materiality filter: only comment on variances above a threshold (default the greater of R10k or 5 percent of the line, state what you used). A long list of tiny variances is noise.
- For each material variance, give the **driver**, not a restatement: not "marketing was R40k over" but "marketing was R40k over because the campaign brought forward from next quarter landed this month, so expect it R40k under next month."
- Separate timing variances (will reverse) from permanent ones (structural). The board cares about the difference.

## Output

- A short table: line, actual, base, variance (rand and percent), for the material lines only.
- Two or three sentences of driver commentary per material variance, timing vs permanent flagged.
- One line on the net effect: is the period genuinely ahead or behind plan once timing is stripped out.
